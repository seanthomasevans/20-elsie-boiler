Venting Detail

Two-pipe system: separate 3" PVC exhaust and 3" PVC intake. Both exit from the top of the Rinnai at 62" AFF, run under the air handler, and penetrate the left exterior brick wall. Not concentric. The RXP199iN is a sealed combustion (direct vent) Category IV appliance.

Side Cross-Section (Looking Left)

Side cross-section showing both vent pipes. Exhaust (orange) exits the top of the Rinnai, runs at 62" AFF along the back wall, and penetrates the exterior brick wall. Intake (teal) exits the top of the Rinnai, jogs 12" forward, and runs parallel to the exhaust through the wall. Both pipes are 3" PVC Schedule 40. Each penetration gets a wall thimble with fire-stop caulk. Horizontal runs slope 1/4" per foot back toward the unit for condensate drainage.

Exterior Wall Face (Two Terminations)

View of the exterior brick wall showing both vent terminations. Exhaust cap (left, orange) expels flue gases. Intake cap (right, teal) draws fresh combustion air. 12" separation between centers. Both must clear 12" below any window, 12" above grade, 6" from inside corners, and 3' from gas meters. Each pipe gets its own termination cap. Reuse existing caps from the previous unit.

Why two-pipe instead of concentric? The RXP199iN supports both concentric and two-pipe venting. Two-pipe is more common in townhouse installs. Both pipes are the same diameter (3" PVC), unlike concentric which uses a 5" outer and 3" inner. The existing PVC from the old unit can be reused with new elbows.
Never mix vent materials. System 636 PVC throughout. Never connect to an existing B-vent or chimney. Use only Rinnai-approved termination caps. Both pipes must have independent wall penetrations with firestop. Condensate is acidic (pH 3-4), route to floor drain via 1/2" NPT at unit bottom. Slope all horizontal runs 1/4" per foot back to unit.
