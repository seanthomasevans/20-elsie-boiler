Installation Notes

1
Wall penetration: Core drill through exterior wall. Install fire-rated wall thimble/escutcheon. Seal gap between thimble and pipe with fire stop caulk. Exterior side gets weather-sealed flashing.
2
Slope requirement: Horizontal vent runs must slope back toward the unit at 1/4" per foot minimum. This allows condensate to drain back to the unit's internal condensate trap rather than pooling in the vent pipe.
3
Condensate drain: The Rinnai produces acidic condensate (pH 3-4). The internal condensate trap connects to a 1/2" NPT drain at the bottom of the unit. Route to a floor drain or condensate pump. Neutralizer kit recommended but not required by Ontario code.
4
Termination cap: Use Rinnai-approved concentric vent termination cap. The cap prevents rain/snow ingestion and maintains proper exhaust/intake separation. Do not substitute generic caps.
5
Sealed combustion: The RXP199iN draws all combustion air through the outer pipe of the concentric vent. No additional combustion air openings needed in the closet. This is why it's safe in an enclosed utility closet.
Critical: Never mix vent pipe materials. System 636 PVC throughout. Never connect to an existing B-vent or chimney. The RXP199iN is a Category IV (positive pressure, condensing) appliance and requires its own dedicated sealed vent system.
