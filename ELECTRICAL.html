Electrical Wiring Diagram

Complete wiring for the hydronic heating control circuit. Shows how the thermostat call propagates through the zone valve end switch to trigger the circulation pump, which creates the flow that activates the Rinnai.

Wire Schedule

From To Wire Voltage Notes
Thermostat Zone valve (V8043E) 18/2 thermostat wire 24V AC R + W terminals. Heat call opens valve motor.
Transformer Zone valve power 18/2 24V AC 40VA transformer powers valve motor. Can be shared with thermostat.
Zone valve end switch Pump relay 18/2 24V AC End switch closes when valve fully open. Energizes relay coil.
Pump relay (line side) Circulation pump 14/2 NMD90 120V AC Relay switches 120V to Grundfos pump. 15A circuit.
Panel Rinnai RXP199iN 14/2 NMD90 120V AC Dedicated 15A circuit. GFCI protected per code.
Panel Air handler (36HX) 14/2 NMD90 120V AC Fan motor power. Can share circuit with pump if within amperage.

Heat Call Sequence

1
Thermostat calls for heat
Room temperature drops below setpoint. Thermostat closes R-W circuit, sending 24V AC to zone valve motor.
2
Zone valve opens
V8043E motor drives valve open (takes about 15 seconds). When fully open, the internal end switch closes.
3
End switch triggers pump relay
End switch sends 24V AC to the relay coil. Relay closes its 120V contacts, powering the Grundfos circulation pump.
4
Circulation pump starts
Grundfos UP 15-42 creates flow through the Rinnai and heating circuit. Flow rate: 3-5 GPM through the fan coil.
5
Rinnai fires on flow detection
The Rinnai's internal flow sensor detects water movement and fires the burner. Heats water to the setpoint (140°F for heating). No external signal needed.
6
Hot water heats the air
140°F water flows through the fan coil in the air handler. Fan blows room air across the coil, warming the space. Return water (~110°F) re-enters the Rinnai cold inlet.
7
Thermostat satisfied, system shuts down
Thermostat opens R-W circuit. Zone valve motor de-energizes and closes. End switch opens, killing relay. Pump stops. Flow ceases. Rinnai stops firing.
Safety: The Rinnai must be on a dedicated 15A GFCI-protected circuit. The 24V control wiring must be separated from 120V power wiring. All 120V connections in junction boxes with wire nuts and proper covers. The pump relay must be rated for the Grundfos inrush current.
Simplicity note: The Rinnai has NO external control wiring for heating. It fires purely on flow detection. This means no aquastat, no relay to the Rinnai, no thermostat interface on the unit itself. The entire control chain is: thermostat opens valve, valve starts pump, pump creates flow, flow activates Rinnai. Elegant.
