20 Elsie Lane #217, Junction, Toronto

## Before You Start

### Permits and Inspections

- **Gas work** requires a licensed G2/G3 gas fitter in Ontario (TSSA regulated). Sean can do the water piping, venting, and electrical, but the gas connection must be done by a licensed tech and inspected.
- **Plumbing permit** may be required by the City of Toronto for hot water heater replacement. Check with Toronto Building at 416-397-5330 or [toronto.ca/building-permits](https://www.toronto.ca/city-government/planning-development/building-permits/).
- **Electrical**: the Rinnai plugs into a standard 120V outlet (3-prong, GFCI recommended). No electrical permit needed if the outlet already exists.

### Safety Warnings

- **Shut off gas** at the main before disconnecting the old unit. Yellow-handle ball valve on the CSST line.
- **Shut off water** at the main supply before cutting any pipes.
- **Drain the old tank** completely before removal. It weighs 100+ lb full.
- **PVC cement fumes are toxic.** Work in ventilation or wear a respirator with organic vapor cartridge.
- **Never test gas connections with a flame.** Use soapy water or a gas leak detector spray.
- **The condensate is acidic** (pH 3-4). Don't let it contact bare metal or skin. Wear gloves when handling.

## Step 1: Remove the Old Unit

**Time: 1-2 hours**

1. **Shut off gas** at the yellow ball valve on the CSST line.
2. **Shut off water** at the main supply valve.
3. **Turn off power** to the old unit and the air handler at the breaker.
4. **Drain the old tank.** Connect a garden hose to the drain valve at the bottom. Run to a floor drain or outside. Open the T&P relief valve on top to break vacuum. Let it drain completely (20-40 min for a 40-gal tank).
5. **Disconnect the gas line.** Use two wrenches: hold the union/fitting with one, turn the other. Cap the gas line with a brass cap and Teflon tape.
6. **Disconnect water lines.** Cut or disconnect at the nearest union or SharkBite fitting. Have a bucket ready for residual water.
7. **Disconnect the vent.** Remove the draft hood and vent connector from the old unit.
8. **Disconnect electrical.** Note the wiring before removing. Take photos. The control board connections between the thermostat, zone valve, and air handler need to be documented.
9. **Remove the old unit.** Two-person job if it's a tank-style unit. Slide it out, don't drop it.
10. **Clean the area.** Sweep out debris, check for water damage, verify the mounting wall is solid.

## Step 2: Mount the Rinnai RXP199iN

**Time: 30-60 minutes**

### Rinnai Clearance Requirements (from installation manual)

| Location | Minimum Clearance |
|---|---|
| Top | 2" (0" from vent components) |
| Bottom (to floor) | 12" |
| Sides (left and right) | 2" |
| Front | 0" (24" recommended for service access) |
| Back | 0" |

### Unit Dimensions
- Width: 18.5" (470 mm)
- Height: 31.3" (795 mm)
- Depth: 11.45" (290 mm)
- Weight: 73 lb (33 kg)

### Mounting Position
- **Center of unit at X = 4" to 22.5"** (left-biased in the 36" closet)
- **Bottom of unit at 24" AFF** (above the 12" minimum clearance)
- **Top of unit at ~55" AFF** (leaving room for vent connections above)
- **Back flush against wall** (0" rear clearance allowed)

### Procedure

1. **Mark the mounting position.** Use a level to draw a plumb line at the center of the unit position (X = 13.25" from left wall). Mark the top of the unit at 55" AFF.
2. **Install the mounting bracket.** The Rinnai comes with a wall bracket. Hold it level against the wall at your marks. Mark the screw holes.
3. **Drill and anchor.** If mounting to concrete or brick, use 1/4" masonry anchors. If drywall over studs, use 1/4" lag bolts into studs. The bracket must support 73 lb.
4. **Hang the unit.** Lift the Rinnai and hook it onto the bracket. Verify it's level and plumb. Snug the security screws.
5. **Verify clearances.** Check 2" minimum on each side, 12" to floor, 2" to ceiling/vent above.

## Step 3: Connect the Venting

**Time: 1-2 hours**

### Specs

- **Vent connection at unit:** 2" (51 mm) nominal, top of unit
- **Approved material (Canada):** ULC S636 certified thermoplastic (IPEX System 636)
- **Configuration:** Direct vent, twin pipe (separate intake + exhaust) through left exterior wall
- **Exhaust slope:** 1/4" per foot toward the unit (so condensate drains back)
- **Intake slope:** 1/4" per foot away from the unit (so rain drains out)
- **Each 90 elbow:** deducts 6 ft from maximum vent run
- **Each 45 elbow:** deducts 3 ft from maximum vent run
- **Maximum run (3" pipe):** 150 ft total equivalent
- **Support:** horizontal runs every 4 ft, vertical runs every 6 ft

### Procedure

1. **Plan the route.** From the top of the Rinnai, exhaust goes up ~6", then 90 elbow to horizontal, then straight through the left (exterior) wall. Intake runs parallel, 12" minimum between exhaust and intake terminations outside.
2. **Upsize to 3".** Install the 2" to 3" reducer at the unit's vent collar. Running 3" gives 150 ft of equivalent length, plenty of margin.
3. **Cut the wall penetration.** Use a 3.5" hole saw for each 3" pipe. Two holes, 12" apart vertically, through the exterior wall. From inside, angle slightly downward (exhaust) or slightly upward (intake).
4. **Dry-fit all vent pieces first.** Assemble without cement to verify fit and slope. Mark alignment lines with a Sharpie across each joint.
5. **Cement the joints.** Apply purple primer to both surfaces (pipe OD and fitting ID). Apply medium-body PVC cement. Insert and twist 1/4 turn. Hold 30 seconds. Allow 2 hours cure before running the unit.
6. **Install wall thimbles.** Seal the wall penetrations with fire-rated thimbles or fire caulk (3M CP 25WB+ or Hilti CFS-S ACR).
7. **Install vent terminations.** Exhaust and intake termination caps on the exterior wall. Minimum clearances (CSA B149.1):
   - 12" above grade
   - 36" from any openable window or door
   - 12" between terminals at the same level
   - 12" above anticipated snow line

## Step 4: Build the Manifold (Water Piping)

**Time: 2-3 hours**

This is the core of the build. All connections use 3/4" CPVC with SharkBite push-to-connect fittings unless noted.

### Connection Order (Bottom of Rinnai, Left to Right)

Looking at the bottom of the unit from the front:

| Position | Connection | Size | Pipe Color |
|---|---|---|---|
| Far left | Gas inlet | 3/4" NPT | Yellow (CSST) |
| Center-left | Cold water inlet | 3/4" NPT | Blue (cold) |
| Center-right | Hot water outlet | 3/4" NPT | Red (hot) |
| Far right | Condensate drain | 1/2" NPT | Green (condensate) |

**Note:** Verify connection positions against your specific unit. The P&R valve connection (M33 thread) is also on the bottom. Install the included T&P relief valve before mounting.

### 4A: Cold Water Supply

1. **From the main cold supply**, run 3/4" CPVC to the Rinnai's cold inlet.
2. **Install a SharkBite ball valve** on the cold supply line for isolation.
3. **Install the Watts PLT-5 expansion tank** on the cold supply, upstream of the Rinnai. Tee off with a SharkBite tee. The expansion tank mounts vertically (connection at top, tank hanging below). Position at X = 29" (right side of closet), approximately 44" AFF.
4. **Pre-charge the expansion tank** to match your incoming water pressure (typically 50-60 PSI in Toronto). Use a bicycle pump or small compressor on the Schrader valve. Check with a tire gauge.

### 4B: Hot Water Outlet (DHW)

1. **From the Rinnai's hot outlet**, run 3/4" CPVC.
2. **Install the TMV (Cash Acme HG110-HX)** downstream of the hot outlet. The TMV has three ports:
   - **Hot input:** from Rinnai hot outlet
   - **Cold input:** branch off the cold supply line (before the Rinnai)
   - **Mixed output:** to the domestic hot water distribution
3. **Set the TMV to 120F** (49C). This blends the 140-160F output down to safe domestic temperature.
4. **Install a SharkBite ball valve** on the mixed output for isolation.

### 4C: Heating Supply (to Air Handler)

1. **Tee off the hot outlet** (before the TMV) with a SharkBite tee. This is the heating supply.
2. **Route 3/4" CPVC** from the tee:
   - Down to manifold height (~19" AFF)
   - Along the back wall to the right
   - Up a riser on the right side
   - Forward to the air handler front face
   - Left to the AH supply port (X = 26")
3. **Install the Honeywell V8043E1020 zone valve** inline on the heating supply. The zone valve opens when the thermostat calls for heat.

### 4D: Heating Return (from Air Handler)

1. **From the air handler return port** (X = 10"), run 3/4" CPVC:
   - Right to the riser
   - Down to the back wall
   - Along the back wall to the left
   - Down to manifold height (~12" AFF)
2. **Install the Grundfos circulation pump** on the return line. Flow direction: from AH back toward the Rinnai cold inlet.
3. **Install the SharkBite check valve** downstream of the pump (prevents thermosiphon when the pump is off).
4. **Tee the return into the cold supply line** upstream of the Rinnai. This is the "open loop": heated water goes through the fan coil, returns, and re-enters the Rinnai as "cold" input to be reheated.

### 4E: Condensate Drain

1. **From the Rinnai's 1/2" condensate port**, run 1/2" PVC or plastic hose.
2. **Route through the Rinnai condensate neutralizer** (804000074). The neutralizer contains calcium carbite media that raises the pH from ~3.4 to neutral before draining.
3. **Drain to the nearest floor drain.** Maintain a continuous downward slope. The drain must be open to atmosphere (not submerged).
4. **Do NOT share the condensate drain with the T&P relief valve discharge.** Separate lines required.

### 4F: Gas Connection

**This step requires a licensed G2/G3 gas fitter.**

1. **Existing 3/4" CSST line** routes to the Rinnai's gas inlet.
2. **Install a drip leg (sediment trap)** below the gas connection: a vertical 3" nipple + cap below the gas shutoff tee. This catches debris and moisture before it enters the unit.
3. **Use yellow Teflon tape + pipe dope** on all gas thread connections.
4. **Leak test every joint** with soapy water spray. Bubbles = leak. Re-tighten or redo.
5. **The gas fitter will verify:** supply pressure (3.5-10.5 in. W.C. for natural gas), line sizing (3/4" is adequate for 199K BTU on a run under 20 ft), and proper shutoff valve operation.

## Step 5: Electrical and Controls

**Time: 1-2 hours**

### Power

- The Rinnai has a **5 ft power cord with 3-prong plug**. Plug into a dedicated 120V, 15A outlet (GFCI recommended).
- **Power consumption:** 84W operating, 1.3W standby, 160W with freeze protection active.
- **No extension cord.** The outlet must be within 5 ft of the unit.

### Thermostat Wiring (Heat Call Sequence)

The existing thermostat wiring stays largely intact. The sequence:

1. **Thermostat calls for heat** (closes R-W circuit)
2. **24V signal energizes the Honeywell zone valve** (V8043E1020)
3. **Zone valve opens fully** (takes ~15 seconds)
4. **Zone valve end switch closes**, powering the Grundfos circulation pump
5. **Pump starts**, flowing water through the heating loop
6. **Rinnai senses flow** through its internal flow switch and fires
7. **Hot water circulates** through the air handler fan coil
8. **Air handler fan runs** (triggered by the aqua-stat or fan relay on the AH control board when water temperature is high enough)
9. **Thermostat satisfied**, zone valve closes, pump stops, Rinnai stops firing

### Wiring Connections

| Wire Run | From | To | Wire |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thermostat R | Thermostat | Zone valve 24V input | 18 AWG, 2-conductor |
| Thermostat W | Thermostat | Zone valve 24V input | 18 AWG, 2-conductor |
| Zone valve end switch | Zone valve | Pump relay (24V terminal block) | 18 AWG, 2-conductor |
| Pump power | 120V outlet or relay | Grundfos pump | As existing |
| AH fan control | AH control board | Fan motor | As existing (don't touch) |
| Rinnai power | 120V outlet | Rinnai (built-in cord) | Factory cord |

### Optional: Rinnai Temperature Controller

- The **MCC-601-W** ($308) mounts on the wall and lets you adjust the Rinnai's output temperature remotely.
- Connect with 2-conductor cable (min 22 AWG) to the controller terminals inside the Rinnai's front panel.
- **Not required** for the system to work. The unit defaults to 120F and can be adjusted via the on-board controls. But for hydronic heating, you may want to set it to 140-160F, which the controller makes easy.

## Step 6: Fill, Purge, and Test

**Time: 1-2 hours**

### Fill and Purge Air

1. **Close all isolation valves** (ball valves on cold in and hot out).
2. **Open the main water supply.**
3. **Slowly open the cold inlet isolation valve.** Listen for water filling the unit.
4. **Open a hot water tap** (bathroom sink is closest). Let it run until a steady stream with no air sputtering comes out. This purges air from the DHW side.
5. **Open the heating loop.** Open the zone valve manually (Honeywell V8043E has a manual lever). Let water flow through the fan coil and back. Open a purge/hose bib on the return line and run water until no air bubbles appear.
6. **Check the expansion tank pressure.** With the system full and cold, the pressure should be at your supply pressure (50-60 PSI). The expansion tank pre-charge should match.

### Leak Test

1. **Pressurize the system** (just turn on the water and let it reach line pressure).
2. **Inspect every joint.** SharkBite fittings, TMV connections, expansion tank tee, zone valve, pump unions, check valve. Look for drips.
3. **Gas leak test.** With gas turned on, spray every gas joint with soapy water. Watch for bubbles for 3 minutes at each joint.
4. **Check condensate drain.** Verify the neutralizer is connected and the drain line runs to the floor drain with slope.

### First Fire

1. **Turn on the gas** at the yellow ball valve.
2. **Plug in the Rinnai.** The display should light up.
3. **Open a hot water tap.** The Rinnai should fire within 3-5 seconds. Verify the flame is visible through the viewport (should be blue with no yellow tips).
4. **Check the flue gas.** Feel the exhaust vent termination outside. Should be warm with no unusual smell.
5. **Test the heating loop.** Set the thermostat to call for heat. The zone valve should open, pump should start, Rinnai should fire. Check that warm air comes from the registers within 2-3 minutes.
6. **Check the condensate flow.** With the unit firing, condensate should begin dripping from the neutralizer within a few minutes.

### Verify Temperatures

| Location | Expected Temperature |
|---|---|
| Rinnai hot outlet (heating mode) | 140-160F |
| TMV mixed output (DHW) | 120F |
| AH supply (heating loop) | 135-155F |
| AH return (heating loop) | 100-120F (delta-T of 20-40F) |
| Domestic hot water at tap | 118-122F |

Use a thermometer or infrared gun to verify.

## Annual Maintenance

| Task | Frequency | How |
|---|---|---|
| Flush heating loop | Annually (fall) | Close zone valve, connect hose to purge valve, run cold water through until clear |
| Replace neutralizer media | Annually | Rinnai 809000114, pour out old media, refill ($25) |
| Clean cold inlet filter | Annually | Remove the inline screen on the Rinnai's cold water inlet. Rinse under tap. |
| Check expansion tank pre-charge | Annually | Isolate tank, depressurize, check Schrader valve with tire gauge. Re-charge to system pressure. |
| Inspect vent terminations | Annually (fall) | Check for obstructions (bird nests, ice, debris). Clear if needed. |
| Descale the heat exchanger | Every 2-3 years (or when error code LC appears) | Pump white vinegar through the unit using a descaling pump kit. 45-minute procedure. |
| Test T&P relief valve | Annually | Lift the lever briefly. Water should flow and stop when released. If it drips continuously, replace. |
| Visual inspection | Annually | Check all connections for corrosion, drips, or discoloration. Check condensate drain is flowing. |

## Troubleshooting Quick Reference

| Symptom | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| No hot water, no flame | Gas shutoff closed, no power, or error code | Check gas valve, power, read error code on display |
| Error code 11 | No ignition | Check gas supply, verify pressure (3.5-10.5 in. W.C.), clean igniter |
| Error code 12 | Flame loss during operation | Check vent for blockage, verify gas pressure, check for wind interference at termination |
| Error code 14 | Thermal fuse triggered (overheat) | Check for flow restriction, verify pump operation, may need reset |
| Error code LC | Scale buildup in heat exchanger | Descale with vinegar flush |
| Heat works but no DHW | TMV set too low or failed | Check TMV setting, verify cold input to TMV is flowing |
| DHW works but no heat | Zone valve not opening | Check thermostat wiring, verify 24V at zone valve, check manual lever |
| Pump runs but no heat | Air locked or zone valve closed | Purge air from heating loop, check zone valve |
| Condensate drain backing up | Clogged neutralizer or drain line | Replace neutralizer media, check drain line slope and for blockages |
| Unit fires then stops quickly | Minimum flow not met (0.4 GPM) | Check for closed valves, clogged filter, or air in lines |
| Vent condensation on wall | Improper vent slope | Re-slope exhaust pipe 1/4" per foot toward unit |
