Manifold & Connections

The manifold zone lives between the Rinnai and the floor (0-24" AFF). This is where all the valves, tees, and routing happen. All joints are SharkBite push-to-connect on 3/4" CPVC. No soldering, no crimping.

Manifold Piping Layout

Click to zoom

Looking at the manifold zone. Hot outlet (red) and cold inlet (blue) come down from the Rinnai bottom connections. The hot side splits: one path to the TMV for DHW, the other through the zone valve and circulation pump for heating. The expansion tank hangs off the cold supply line. Heating return (green) reconnects to cold supply through a check valve. Isolation ball valves on every major branch.

TMV-1: Mixing Valve
Cash Acme HG110-HX
On hot outlet, downstream of heating tee. Blends 140°F down to 120°F for DHW fixtures.
ET-1: Expansion Tank
Watts PLT-5 (2.1 gal, potable)
Tees off the cold supply line, hangs below. Pre-charge to match street pressure. Single tank for entire system.
ZV-1: Zone Valve
Honeywell V8043E
On heating supply branch. Opens on thermostat call. Internal end switch triggers pump relay when fully open.
CP-1: Circulation Pump
Grundfos UP 15-42 (1/25 HP)
Downstream of zone valve on heating supply. Creates the flow that activates the Rinnai. 3-speed.
CV-1: Check Valve
SharkBite 3/4" swing check
On heating return. Prevents cold supply from flowing backward through the fan coil when heating is off.
BV 1-4: Isolation Valves
SharkBite 3/4" full port
Cold inlet, hot outlet, heating supply, heating return. Allows servicing any component without full shutdown.

SharkBite Push-to-Connect Detail

Click to zoom

Every joint in the manifold uses SharkBite push-to-connect fittings on CPVC pipe. The fitting contains an O-ring seal (watertight), stainless steel teeth (grip the pipe), and a release collar (push to disconnect). Mark the insertion depth on the pipe, deburr the cut end, push until it clicks. Rated for both exposed and concealed applications. Use a tubing cutter for square cuts, never a hacksaw.

Assembly order matters. Work from the Rinnai connections downward. Connect hot outlet tee first (DHW + heating split). Then cold inlet with expansion tank tee. Then zone valve and pump on heating branch. Then check valve on return. Then isolation valves on each branch. Test each joint with system pressure before moving to the next. 40 PSI static test minimum.
