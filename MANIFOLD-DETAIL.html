Manifold Piping Detail

Detailed layout of the manifold zone between the Rinnai and the floor. Shows pipe routing, valve positions, tee locations, and component heights. All connections are 3/4" CPVC with SharkBite push-to-connect fittings.

Valve & Component Schedule

TMV-1: Mixing Valve
Cash Acme HG110-HX
Blends Rinnai output (140°F) down to 120°F for DHW fixtures. Installed on hot outlet, downstream of heating tee.
Hot side
ET-1: Expansion Tank
Watts PLT-5 (2.1 gal)
Potable-rated thermal expansion tank on cold supply line. Pre-charge to match street pressure. Single tank for entire system.
Cold side
ZV-1: Zone Valve
Honeywell V8043E
Motorized zone valve on heating supply to fan coil. Opens on thermostat call. End switch triggers pump relay.
Heating circuit
CP-1: Circulation Pump
Grundfos UP 15-42
3-speed circulator on heating supply line, downstream of zone valve. Starts when ZV-1 end switch closes. 1/25 HP.
Heating circuit
BV-1..4: Isolation Valves
SharkBite 3/4" full port
Ball valves at cold inlet, hot outlet, heating supply, and heating return. Allows servicing any component without full shutdown.
All circuits
CV-1: Check Valve
SharkBite 3/4" swing check
On heating return line. Prevents cold supply water from flowing backward through the fan coil when heating is off.
Heating return

Flow Logic

Cold supply enters
Municipal water through BV-1 isolation valve. Expansion tank ET-1 tees off here. Continues to Rinnai cold inlet.
Hot output splits at tee
Rinnai hot outlet exits at 140°F. First tee sends water to heating circuit (through ZV-1). Remaining flow continues to TMV-1 for DHW tempering.
Heating circuit runs to fan coil
Hot water through ZV-1, then CP-1 pump pushes it up to the air handler at 64" AFF. Returns through check valve CV-1 back to cold supply tee.
DHW exits to fixtures
TMV-1 blends hot (140°F) with cold bypass to deliver 120°F to all fixtures through BV-2 isolation valve.
Fittings note: All joints are SharkBite push-to-connect on CPVC pipe. No soldering, no crimping. Deburr pipe ends, mark insertion depth, push until click. SharkBite fittings are rated for buried and concealed applications. Use SharkBite disconnect tongs for removal if needed. Ensure pipe is cut square with a tubing cutter, not a hacksaw.
