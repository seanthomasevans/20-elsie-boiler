Piping & Instrumentation Diagram

Complete system schematic showing both the domestic hot water (DHW) loop and the open-loop hydronic heating circuit. This is an open-loop system: potable water flows directly through the fan coil. There is no heat exchanger, no glycol, no secondary loop.

System Schematic (Open-Loop)

Click to zoom

Read left to right: Cold supply (blue) enters through isolation valve, tees to expansion tank below, continues to Rinnai. Hot output splits at a tee: upper path through TMV to DHW fixtures (red), lower path through zone valve and circulation pump to the fan coil air handler. Return (green) from fan coil connects back to cold supply upstream of the Rinnai. Gas (yellow) enters from left.

Thermostatic Mixing Valve (TMV) Detail

Click to zoom

The TMV blends Rinnai output (140°F) with cold bypass water to deliver 120°F to DHW fixtures. An internal thermostatic wax element automatically adjusts the mix ratio. The heating circuit tees off before the TMV, so the fan coil receives full 140°F water. Adjustment dial allows setpoint tuning between 80-160°F.

Open-loop means no heat exchanger. If you see "heat exchanger" in any AI-generated diagram image, ignore it. This system sends potable water directly through the fan coil. That's what makes it simpler, cheaper, and more efficient than a combi boiler with a plate heat exchanger. Rinnai TB-043 bulletin explicitly approves this configuration.
