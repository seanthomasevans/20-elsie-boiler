## Sensitivity Analysis

### What if gas prices increase 20%?

Gas savings from efficiency would increase from $15/month to $18/month. DIY monthly drops to -$12.
- 80-month DIY total: $2,139 (saves $2,661 vs rental)
- Break-even moves earlier to month 46

### What if Enbridge rental is $50/month (not $60)?

- 80-month rental total: $4,000
- DIY savings: $1,621
- Break-even moves to month 62

### What if Enbridge rental is $75/month?

- 80-month rental total: $6,000
- DIY savings: $3,621
- Break-even moves to month 41

### What if the Grundfos pump needs replacement (year 3)?

Add ~$200 for a Taco 007e or equivalent.
- 80-month DIY total: $2,579
- Still saves $2,221 vs rental

### What if the Rinnai heat exchanger fails after warranty (year 13)?

Outside the 80-month window. But replacement HX is ~$800-1,200 + labor. Still cheaper than cumulative rental over 13 years ($9,360-11,700 in rental fees alone).

---

## The Math

Over 80 months, Sean's DIY build saves **$2,421** vs continuing the Enbridge rental and **$7,791** vs hiring a pro to install a combi boiler. The break-even against the rental is **month 52** (just over 4 years). After break-even, the system effectively pays Sean **$69/month** (the eliminated rental fee plus gas savings).

The DIY approach is the clear financial winner. The only scenario where it loses is if a major component fails in the first 2 years, and even then the math barely changes because every individual component is a $50-200 commodity part.
