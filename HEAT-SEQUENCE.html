Hydronic Heat Call Sequence

How a thermostat call for heat propagates through the system. From temperature drop to warm air, every step in the chain. The beauty of this design is that the Rinnai requires zero external control wiring. It fires purely on flow detection.

Time to heat
~30 seconds from thermostat call to warm air
Control signals
24V AC thermostat wire + flow activation (no Rinnai wiring)
Water temp (heating)
140°F supply / ~110°F return
Flow rate
3-5 GPM through fan coil
1

Thermostat calls for heat

Room temperature drops below the thermostat setpoint. The thermostat closes the R-W circuit, sending 24V AC down the thermostat wire to the zone valve motor.

Thermostat
2

Zone valve opens

The Honeywell V8043E receives the 24V signal and its motor drives the valve open. This takes approximately 15 seconds. When the valve reaches fully open, an internal end switch mechanically closes.

Honeywell V8043E
3

End switch triggers pump relay

The V8043E end switch sends 24V AC to a relay coil. The relay closes its 120V contacts, completing the power circuit to the circulation pump. This two-stage design isolates the low-voltage control from the line-voltage pump.

24V Relay
4

Circulation pump starts

The Grundfos UP 15-42 starts circulating water through the heating loop. Water flows from the Rinnai hot outlet, through the zone valve, through the pump, up to the fan coil air handler, and returns to the Rinnai cold inlet via the check valve.

Grundfos UP 15-42
5

Rinnai fires on flow detection

The Rinnai's internal flow sensor detects water movement (minimum activation: 0.4 GPM). The burner ignites and heats the water to the setpoint (140°F for heating mode). No external signal, no aquastat, no relay to the Rinnai needed. It's purely flow-activated.

Rinnai RXP199iN
6

Hot water heats the air

140°F water flows through the copper coil inside the First Company 36HX air handler. The fan blows room air across the coil, transferring heat. Return water exits the fan coil at approximately 110°F and re-enters the Rinnai cold inlet through the check valve.

First Company 36HX
7

Thermostat satisfied

Room reaches setpoint. Thermostat opens the R-W circuit. Zone valve motor de-energizes and spring-closes the valve. End switch opens, killing the relay. Pump stops. Flow ceases. Rinnai's flow sensor detects no flow and the burner shuts off. System at rest.

Full shutdown
Why this works: The Rinnai RXP199iN fires on any flow above 0.4 GPM. There's no "heating mode" input or external aquastat needed. The circulation pump creates enough flow to activate the burner. The Rinnai doesn't know or care whether it's heating DHW or a hydronic loop. It just heats whatever water flows through it. This is what makes the open-loop design so simple and reliable.
